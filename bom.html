§ 2Component guide with suppliers

2.1 Solar cells — the cost driver

Spec to order: M10 mono TOPCon (or PERC), half-cut, one current bin, one color class, with datasheet Voc/Isc/Vmp/Imp per cell — those numbers feed SC-01 directly. TOPCon is now the mainstream cell; PERC is cheaper and slightly easier to hand-solder (more forgiving metallization).

Cell buying rules Never mix bins in one module. Ask for the cell datasheet + EL sample images of the lot. Cells ship in foam-and-vacuum bricks; refuse visibly re-packed bricks. Air freight for prototype lots is fine (cells are light); glass is what forces sea freight.

§ 3The Philippines sourcing reality

  • There is no local module-materials supply chain. Cells, EVA, backsheet, solar glass, and J-boxes are all imports, effectively all from China. PH-local wins are: tempered low-iron glass (architectural shops), aluminum extrusion, sealants, packaging/crating, and general electronics consumables (flux, wire, connectors — Raon district, RS Components PH, online industrial suppliers).
  • Prototype lots: order consolidated via one Alibaba trader or a materials-kit vendor so everything but glass arrives in one air/sea shipment. Glass: source locally first (see 2.3) to avoid crating a single pane across the sea.
  • Import mechanics: small lots clear as ordinary imports (duty + 12% VAT on CIF). At production scale, DOE accreditation + BOI registration can make manufacturing inputs duty- and VAT-exempt under RA 9513 §21 — the numbers and process are in SC-04 §3.
  • Freight rule of thumb: cells and sheet goods air-freight economically for prototypes; glass, frames, and anything at batch scale go LCL/FCL sea to Manila.
