## The problem for Biliran families

- **Power is expensive here:** BILECO residential runs ≈ **₱12.95/kWh (2026)** — higher than
  mainland Leyte.
- **Brownouts are routine** on the island grid — and during typhoons, public facilities that
  matter most (evacuation centers, health stations, water pumps) go dark.
- **Solar help comes from off-island** — Tacloban or Cebu installers — adding freight cost,
  slow warranty service, and money leaving the province.

## What we propose (three parts, all legal, mostly no cost to the LGU)

**1. Energy-resilience pilot — solarize one public facility.**
An evacuation center, barangay hall, rural health unit, or water pump fitted with a
typhoon-rated solar + battery system that keeps lights, comms, and refrigeration running
through a brownout or after a storm. Delivered either through **lawful public bidding
(RA 9184)** or as a **sponsored CSR install at cost** — the LGU chooses.

**2. Local jobs and skills.**
We register and pay business tax in Biliran, and **hire and train local residents as
certified solar installers** — keeping the work, the warranty service, and the wages on the
island instead of importing crews.

**3. Affordable home solar + BILECO paperwork handled.**
Fixed-price ₱99,500 household packages, with **we-handle-the-BILECO-net-metering** service,
so ordinary families can cut their bill and ride out brownouts. We run barangay
energy-resilience demo days to show real, touchable equipment.

## What we ask of the LGU (non-monetary)

- **Endorsement / permission** to hold barangay or HOA demo days.
- **Inclusion** in the municipality's disaster-preparedness and livelihood programs.
- **A fair, open bidding process** for any public-facility solar work — awarded on merit.
- **Support spreading net-metering and energy-resilience awareness** with BILECO and the
  Sangguniang Bayan.

## What Biliran gets

- Public facilities that **stay powered** during the next typhoon or brownout.
- **Local green jobs** and skills that stay in the province.
- A **homegrown RE champion** and lower long-term community power costs.
- If the pilot is CSR: **at no cost to the municipality.**

## Our integrity commitment

All government work goes through **lawful public procurement**, awarded on merit — no private
arrangements, no gifts, no commissions to any official. We compete to be the **best local
bid**, not a favored one.
