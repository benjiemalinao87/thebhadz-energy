## 1. The market shape — an underserved island

- **Biliran is a small island province**, sole distributor **BILECO** (Biliran Electric
  Cooperative), 117 barangays across 7 municipalities, HQ in Caraycaray, Naval.
- **BILECO residential rate: ₱12.9458/kWh (2026)**, category range ~₱12.64–15.09/kWh —
  **higher than nearby Leyte** (LEYECO 4 ≈ ₱10.5/kWh) and squarely in Meralco territory.
  Island power is expensive.
- **Frequent brownouts** — island coop grid, scheduled interruptions are routine. This is
  the #1 organic demand driver.
- **No confirmed solar installer physically based in Biliran.** The serving competition is
  all off-island — Tacloban (road) or Cebu (barge/air freight). **That absence is the
  opening.**

> **Strategic consequence:** this is a textbook **Big Fish, Small Pond** situation
> (Obviously Awesome §3). We can't out-track-record a 20-year Luzon installer nationally —
> but on Biliran island, the pond is winnable because nobody local is in it. The wedge is
> **being the on-island installer**, not being the cheapest panel.

## 4. Where we win — the Biliran wedge (divergence, not parity)

**Table stakes we simply match, no debate** (§3): tier-1 IEC-certified panels, honored
warranty, **typhoon-rated mounting**, permits + **BILECO** net-metering handled, anti-scam
trust signals. Above that line, our divergence (each item is an ERRC trade-off, §3):

1. **On-island presence eliminates the freight surcharge and the warranty-across-the-strait
   delay.** Off-island installers either pad the quote for barge/air freight or won't come
   for a single residential job. We're already here. *(Raises: service speed, trust.
   Eliminates: freight cost, remote-warranty risk.)*
2. **We own the BILECO net-metering paperwork** — a specific, local, bureaucratic moat.
   National installers know Meralco, not BILECO. *(Unblocks the purchase/delivery stage.)*
3. **Typhoon-rated racking lands hardest here.** Eastern Visayas is the highest
   typhoon-awareness market in the country (Yolanda; 150 kph / IEC-61215 mounting is the
   regional expectation). Our fabricated typhoon-rated racking (SC-05 §5) is the physical
   proof — and it directly answers the local fear that a cheap Shopee-kit roof mount peels
   off in the first signal-3 storm. *(Raises: durability. This is our headline, not watts.)*
4. **Fixed ₱99,500 all-in price** in a market used to opaque, freight-inflated quotes —
   with the "reason why" it's lower (fixed package, standardized BOM, no showroom, no barge).

## 5. What we copy vs what we never copy (§4)

- **Copy the mechanics, no debate:** *where and how* the regional players sell — Facebook
  pages (RK Energy), Viber/WhatsApp quote threads (SolarStream), the "free site survey"
  offer, bundle pricing. Their spend is free market research (§4).
- **Diverge on the promise:** our value curve (local + typhoon-rated + BILECO-handled +
  fixed price), never their feature list.
- **Never copy competitor claims:** their savings/backup numbers aren't held to our §1.6
  honesty bar. We state only what our package delivers.
