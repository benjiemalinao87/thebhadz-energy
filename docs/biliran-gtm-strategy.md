**What we are:** a fixed-price ₱99,500 home solar package — installed, permitted,

**The pond (Big Fish, Small Pond):** fixed-price home solar in the ₱80–100k window, *on

**The wedge (divergence, not ₱/W parity):**

2. **BILECO-fluent** — we own the net-metering paperwork; national installers know Meralco.
3. **Typhoon-rated racking** — Eastern Visayas is the most typhoon-aware market (Yolanda);
   our fabricated racking is the physical proof. The headline, not watts.
4. **Fixed ₱99,500 all-in** — against opaque, freight-inflated quotes, with the "reason why."

**Table stakes we simply match:** tier-1 certified panels, honored warranty, typhoon
mounting, permits/net-metering handled, anti-scam trust signals.

## 7. Local government & community engagement — the LEGAL way

The instinct to get LGU backing that favors a homegrown Biliran company over off-island
installers is sound. We earn it by **being the local economic story**, never by paying for
preference.

**Do (legal, and usually stronger):**
- **Be genuinely local:** register the business in Naval, pay local business tax, **hire and
  train Biliran installers.** A mayor can publicly champion local jobs and tax base for free
  — that is "incentivize local over outside," done lawfully.
- **Solarize a public facility** — evacuation center, barangay hall, health station, water
  pump — via **proper public procurement (RA 9184 bidding)** *or* sponsored CSR at cost. A
  solar-powered evacuation center in a post-Yolanda province is a legal, highly visible
  proof point and community goodwill.
- **Ask for non-monetary support:** LGU endorsement/permission to run a barangay demo day;
  inclusion in a municipal **disaster-preparedness / livelihood** program; help spreading
  net-metering and energy-resilience awareness with the Sangguniang Bayan.
- **Work the utility, not the official:** partner with **BILECO** on net-metering education —
  a legitimate, above-board relationship.

**Never (hard stop — legality is non-overridable):**
- **No payment, percentage, commission, gift, or kickback to any public official** (mayor,
  councilor, BILECO officer) in their official capacity. That is bribery under **RA 3019,
  RA 6713, and the Revised Penal Code** — criminal for both giver and receiver.
- Beyond the law: in a scam-wary market, a "they paid off the mayor" rumor **destroys the
  trust wedge** this entire strategy is built on (§1.6). It's strategically self-defeating,
  not just illegal.
- Any government-facility work goes through **lawful public bidding**, awarded on merit —
  never a private arrangement.

> If an official ever requests a cut, that is a red flag to walk away and document, not a
> cost of doing business. The legal local-preference lever is procurement law + being the
> best local bid, not a personal payment.
