## 1. Competitive alternatives (what buyers use instead — must include non-solar)
**Market: Biliran province (BILECO territory).** See `docs/biliran-competitor-strategy.md`.

- **(a) Do nothing** — keep paying the BILECO bill (≈₱12.95/kWh; category range
  ₱12.64–15.09, 2026) and keep enduring the brownouts.
- **(b) Genset, UPS or AVR** — the brownout stopgap most households already own something of.
- **(c) Off-island established installer** — freight surcharge plus warranty and service that
  has to cross the strait.
  - SolarStream (Tuburan, Cebu): 3 kW hybrid **₱150–200k**, 6 kW **₱330–450k**. Lists
    Leyte/Samar; **Biliran is not in their coverage list.**
  - RK Energy (Tacloban): ~2–3 hr by road plus the bridge. **Price unknown — priority recon.**
  - National brands (Solaric, PHILERGY, LakaSolar, GoSolar): **₱140k–₱900k+**, weak on-island
    presence, competing on web SEO rather than local trust.
  - Established-installer entry point sits at **₱150–280k** — roughly ₱50k+ above our flagship.
- **(d) Shopee/Lazada kit + local freelance electrician** — cheapest hardware, no workmanship
  warranty, no BILECO paperwork, no typhoon-rated mounting.
- **(e) Portable power stations — NEW, and the alternative sitting inside our price window.**
  - Anker SOLIX "Whole-day Back up" bundle **₱94,980** · "Home Back-up Solar" **₱62,180** ·
    C1000 Gen 2 **₱47,990** (5-year local warranty).
  - EcoFlow DELTA 3 Ultra **₱96,990** · DELTA 3 + 220 W **₱77,790** · + 160 W **₱73,290**.
  - Jackery entering PH (Feb 2026) via Cognetics — press-reported, unconfirmed on their own site.
- **No confirmed installer based on Biliran itself — the uncontested local pond.**
  _(Caveat: an unverified "Naval Biliran Solar Power" social signal exists. If it is real, the
  uncontested-pond premise below breaks and this canvas needs a rewrite — confirm first.)_

### Why (e) changes the copy (read before writing any ad)
A homeowner with ₱95k and a brownout problem can buy an Anker or EcoFlow bundle **today** and
have backup this week, with a recognised brand and a 5-year warranty. We cannot beat that on
delivery speed, brand recognition, or warranty paperwork — and we should stop trying.

What those units **do not** do: reduce the monthly BILECO bill *at all*, qualify for
net-metering, mount to a roof through a typhoon, or scale beyond a few appliances. That is the
ERRC line. We are not selling a bigger battery — we are selling **a permanently lower bill and
a roof that keeps working.** Any ad that leads with "backup power" invites the one comparison
we lose twice over. _[HYPOTHESIS — test in interviews: has anyone in the target barangays
actually bought one of these?]_

## 5. Market category (the pond — Big Fish, Small Pond, §3)
- **Fixed-price home solar packages inside the ₱80–100k window.**
- **Corridor sanity check** against the workbook's own benchmarks: the provincial on-grid band
  runs ₱33,500–38,000/kW, which puts ₱99,500 at roughly **2.6–3.0 kWp** of on-grid capacity.
  Red flags either side: above ~₱100/Wp buyers scrutinise; below ~₱60/Wp something is missing
  or non-Tier-1. Confirm against the actual BOM before this number goes in front of anyone.
  Price is set **from the corridor** and the BOM target-costed to it — never cost-plus (§3).
- LIWANAG / ILAW / SANDIGAN are the ladder within the pond. No new-category play until the
  pond is won AND the IEC cert exists.

## 6. Trend (optional, last — only after "what is this?" is answered)
- **Correction (2026-07-22): "Meralco keeps rising" is the wrong why-now for this market.**
  Our buyers are on **BILECO**, not Meralco. BILECO at ≈₱12.95/kWh is *not* above the cited
  Meralco range (₱13.47–14.83, Nov 2025–Apr 2026). It **is** well above neighbouring LEYECO 4
  (≈₱10.5/kWh). So the honest local framing is **"we pay more than Leyte and the power still
  goes out"** — not "Meralco keeps rising." _[HYPOTHESIS — confirm the rate with BILECO
  directly before it appears in any copy.]_
- Brownout frequency, not the tariff, is the likelier why-now here. Test which one homeowners
  raise unprompted (§2: ask about the past, never the future).
