# By-Laws of [COMPANY NAME], Inc.

**Template only — not legal advice.** Structured on the standard Philippine SEC by-laws
format for a domestic stock corporation under the Revised Corporation Code (RA 11232).
Fill every `[BRACKETED]` field, delete this notice, and have a lawyer or SEC-accredited
filer review before notarizing/submitting via eSPARC. Not required for a One Person
Corporation (OPCs are exempt from filing by-laws).

## ARTICLE I — Offices

**Section 1. Principal Office.** The principal office of the corporation shall be
located at [FULL REGISTERED ADDRESS, CITY/MUNICIPALITY, PROVINCE, PHILIPPINES], or at
such other place as the Board of Directors may determine, subject to notice to the
Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) as required by law.

## ARTICLE II — Meetings of Stockholders

**Section 1. Annual Meeting.** The annual meeting of stockholders shall be held on
[DATE/MONTH, e.g., "the second Monday of March each year"] at the principal office of
the corporation or such other place within the Philippines as the Board may designate,
for the election of directors and the transaction of such other business as may
properly come before the meeting.

**Section 2. Special Meetings.** Special meetings of stockholders may be called at any
time by the Board of Directors, the President, or upon written request of stockholders
representing at least a majority of the outstanding capital stock.

**Section 3. Notice of Meetings.** Written notice stating the place, date, time, and
purpose(s) of any stockholders' meeting shall be sent to each stockholder of record at
least [21] days prior to the meeting, unless a shorter period is allowed by law and
stockholders holding a majority of outstanding capital stock consent in writing.

**Section 4. Quorum.** Unless otherwise provided by law, a quorum for stockholders'
meetings shall consist of stockholders representing a majority of the outstanding
capital stock, present in person or by proxy.

**Section 5. Voting.** Each share of stock shall be entitled to one vote, except as
otherwise provided in the Articles of Incorporation. Stockholders may vote in person,
by proxy, or through remote communication/in absentia as may be authorized by the Board
and permitted by the Revised Corporation Code.

## ARTICLE III — Board of Directors

**Section 1. Number and Qualifications.** The corporation shall be governed by a Board
of Directors consisting of [NUMBER, e.g., "3"] members, each of whom must own at least
one share of stock in their own name and possess the qualifications required by the
Revised Corporation Code.

**Section 2. Term.** Directors shall be elected at each annual meeting and shall hold
office for one year and until their successors are elected and qualified.

**Section 3. Regular and Special Meetings.** Regular meetings of the Board shall be
held [FREQUENCY, e.g., "quarterly"], on such dates as the Board may fix. Special
meetings may be called by the President or by a majority of the directors, with at
least [5] days' prior notice, unless waived.

**Section 4. Quorum and Voting.** A majority of the number of directors as fixed in
these by-laws shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business. Except where
a greater vote is required by law or the Articles of Incorporation, the vote of a
majority of directors present at a meeting with quorum shall be the act of the Board.
Directors may participate in meetings via remote communication as permitted by law.

**Section 5. Vacancies.** Any vacancy in the Board other than by removal or expiration
of term may be filled by a vote of at least a majority of the remaining directors, if
still constituting a quorum, in accordance with the Revised Corporation Code.

**Section 6. Powers.** The Board shall exercise the corporate powers, conduct all
business, and control all property of the corporation, subject to the Articles of
Incorporation, these by-laws, and applicable law.

## ARTICLE IV — Officers

**Section 1. Officers.** The officers of the corporation shall be a President (who must
be a director), a Corporate Secretary (who must be a Filipino citizen and a resident of
the Philippines), a Treasurer (who must be a resident of the Philippines), and such
other officers as the Board may appoint, including a Compliance Officer if required by
applicable SEC rules. One person may hold two or more positions, except that no one
shall act as both President and Treasurer, or President and Secretary, concurrently
unless the corporation qualifies as and elects to operate as a One Person Corporation.

**Section 2. Election and Term.** Officers shall be elected by the Board immediately
after the annual stockholders' meeting and shall serve for one year and until their
successors are elected and qualified, unless removed earlier for cause.

**Section 3. President.** The President shall preside at all stockholders' and Board
meetings, oversee the general management of the corporation's business, and sign
contracts and other instruments on behalf of the corporation as authorized by the
Board.

**Section 4. Corporate Secretary.** The Corporate Secretary shall keep the minutes of
all stockholders' and Board meetings, maintain the stock and transfer book, issue
notices of meetings, certify corporate records, and perform such other duties as the
Board directs.

**Section 5. Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall have custody of corporate funds and
securities, keep full accounts of receipts and disbursements, render financial
statements as required, and perform such other duties as the Board directs.

## ARTICLE V — Stock Certificates

**Section 1. Form.** Certificates of stock shall be issued in such form as the Board
approves, signed by the President and the Secretary, and bearing the corporate seal (if
any), specifying the number of shares and stating that the shares are fully paid.

**Section 2. Transfer.** Shares of stock shall be transferable only on the books of the
corporation upon surrender of the certificate properly endorsed, and no transfer shall
be valid, except as between the parties, until recorded in the stock and transfer book.

**Section 3. Lost or Destroyed Certificates.** A new certificate may be issued in lieu
of one lost, stolen, or destroyed, upon such terms and proof as the Board requires and
in compliance with the Revised Corporation Code.

## ARTICLE VI — Fiscal Year and Dividends

**Section 1. Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the corporation shall begin on [DATE,
e.g., "January 1"] and end on [DATE, e.g., "December 31"] of each year, unless changed
by Board resolution and duly reported to the BIR and SEC.

**Section 2. Dividends.** Dividends shall be declared and paid out of unrestricted
retained earnings, as and when the Board determines, subject to the Revised Corporation
Code and applicable tax rules.

## ARTICLE VII — Corporate Seal

The corporation may adopt a corporate seal, which shall bear the corporation's full
name, the year of incorporation, and the words "Philippines." Use of a seal is
optional under the Revised Corporation Code but may still be requested by banks or
counterparties.

## ARTICLE VIII — Amendments

These by-laws may be amended or repealed, or new by-laws adopted, by a majority vote of
the Board of Directors and the vote or written assent of stockholders representing at
least a majority of the outstanding capital stock, at a meeting called for that
purpose, subject to filing the amendment with the SEC.

## ARTICLE IX — Effectivity

These by-laws shall take effect upon approval by the SEC as part of the corporation's
registration, or upon subsequent SEC approval if adopted or amended after
incorporation.
