SC-08 · BILIRAN COMPETITOR STRATEGY · REV A · 2026-07-19

Win the island before anyone plants a flag on it

Our actual target market is Biliran province (BILECO territory) — not generic "PH homeowners." The research says it is an uncontested local pond: expensive island power, frequent brownouts, and no confirmed installer based on the island. We don't win on ₱/W against national brands; we win by being the trusted, typhoon-hardened, BILECO-fluent installer who is already here.

Evidence status Competitor reach and prices below are web-sourced (2026-07-19) and HYPOTHESIS until a founder ground-truths them on the island. The recon that confirms them is in docs/biliran-recon-toolkit.md; the full analysis in docs/biliran-competitor-strategy.md.

§ 1The market shape

  • BILECO is the sole distributor — 117 barangays, 7 municipalities, HQ in Caraycaray, Naval.
  • Residential rate ≈ ₱12.9458/kWh (2026) EST, category range ~₱12.64–15.09 — higher than nearby Leyte (LEYECO 4 ≈ ₱10.5/kWh) and Meralco-level. Island power is expensive; that is the bill-pain we sell against.
  • Frequent brownouts — island coop grid, scheduled interruptions routine. The #1 organic demand driver.
  • No confirmed installer based on Biliran. Serving competition is off-island (Tacloban by road, Cebu by barge/air freight). That absence is the opening.

§ 5Copy vs never-copy

  • Copy the mechanics, no debate: where and how they sell — Facebook pages, Viber/WhatsApp quote threads, free-survey offers, bundle pricing. Their spend is free market research.
  • Diverge on the promise: our value curve (local + typhoon-rated + BILECO-handled + fixed price), never their feature list.
  • Never copy competitor claims: their savings/backup numbers aren't held to our honesty bar. We state only what our package delivers.
