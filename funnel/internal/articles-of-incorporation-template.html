Articles of Incorporation of [COMPANY NAME], Inc.

Template only — not legal advice. Structured on the standard Philippine SEC Articles of Incorporation format for a domestic stock corporation under the Revised Corporation Code (RA 11232). eSPARC generates the Articles from your form answers, so treat this as the worksheet for preparing every answer in advance — or as the draft for the notarized PDF upload route. Fill every [BRACKETED] field, delete this notice, and have a lawyer or SEC-accredited filer review before submitting.

FIRST — Corporate Name

The name of the corporation shall be [COMPANY NAME], INC. (verify availability via the eSPARC name-reservation step first; have 2 backup names ready).

SECOND — Purpose

Primary purpose: To engage in the manufacture, assembly, purchase, sale, distribution, installation, and servicing of solar power systems, solar panels, inverters, batteries, mounting structures, and related electrical and renewable-energy equipment; and to provide electrical and renewable-energy installation contracting and maintenance services, in accordance with applicable law and licensing requirements.

Secondary purpose(s): [OPTIONAL — e.g., "To purchase, lease, or otherwise acquire real or personal property necessary for the conduct of the foregoing business." Delete if none.]

THIRD — Principal Office

The principal office of the corporation is located at [FULL REGISTERED ADDRESS, BARANGAY, CITY/MUNICIPALITY, PROVINCE, ZIP], Philippines. (Must match the address used later for BIR and LGU registration.)

FOURTH — Term

The corporation shall have perpetual existence (the default under RA 11232; state a fixed term only if the founders deliberately want one).

SEVENTH — Capital Stock

(RA 11232 removed the old 25%-subscribed / 25%-paid minimum for most corporations, but banks and some regulators still expect meaningful paid-up capital — decide the numbers with the §0 entity decision, and check foreign-ownership capital floors below.)

Foreign-equity check (do this before fixing the table above): if any subscriber is not a Filipino citizen, confirm (a) the activity's ceiling in the current Foreign Investment Negative List, (b) the paid-in capital floor for a >40% foreign-owned domestic-market enterprise (US$200,000, reducible to US$100,000 with advanced technology or ≥15 direct Filipino employees — verify current thresholds), and (c) the PCAB regular contractor license's ≥60% Filipino-ownership requirement, which binds the install-contracting business specifically. A natural-born Filipino who reacquired citizenship under RA 9225 counts as Filipino. Flag any of these for a lawyer before filing.

NINTH — Treasurer

[TREASURER NAME] has been elected by the subscribers as Treasurer of the corporation, to act as such until a successor is duly elected and qualified, and is authorized to receive for the corporation all subscriptions paid by the subscribers.

TENTH — Undertaking to Change Name

The incorporators undertake to change the corporate name if it is found to be identical or deceptively/confusingly similar to a name already registered or protected by law (eSPARC includes this undertaking in its generated form).

A signatory who signs in the United States (or anywhere abroad) must have the document apostilled (or consularized where the apostille convention doesn't apply) for it to be accepted in the Philippines — plan courier time for this if any founder signs from the US.
