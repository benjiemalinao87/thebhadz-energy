SC-14 · ERC & NET METERING · REV A · 2026-07-23

ERC, net metering & renewable-energy regulation (Philippines)

Regulatory reference for the permit / net-metering checklist that must be attached to every quote before a deposit is accepted (Founder OS §7). Covers who regulates what, the legal basis for net metering, the 2025–2026 process reforms, the end-to-end application flow, and what each rule means per package. This is the compliance backbone of the "permits and net-metering handled" table-stakes promise in every BADJJ offer.

Research-dated 2026-07-23 — verify before quoting a customer Timelines, caps, and fees below are compiled from public sources as of 2026-07-23. Rules are moving fast (three major reforms in the last 10 months). Verify against erc.gov.ph, doe.gov.ph, and the local distribution utility (Meralco / BILECO) before committing a date or peso figure to a homeowner. EST

§ 0Who regulates what

Four bodies touch a residential solar install — know which one owns each step
BodyRoleTouches our install where
DOE (Department of Energy) Policy. Issues circulars that set program rules and deadlines for everyone below. Net-metering program rules, the 10-working-day DU approval mandate, capacity caps.
ERC (Energy Regulatory Commission) Independent regulator under EPIRA (RA 9136, 2001). Sets/approves rates, licenses generators (Certificates of Compliance), writes the binding rules DUs must follow, enforces RPS. Net-metering implementing rules (Resolution No. 09-2013), the export-credit rate, the simplified four-document requirement, the CoC application fee.
DU (distribution utility — Meralco, BILECO, other ECs) Franchise holder for the wires. Processes the net-metering application, swaps the meter, signs the net-metering agreement, credits exports on the bill. The application itself. RA 9513 §10 mandates the DU to accommodate a compliant application — it cannot refuse one.
LGU (city/municipal engineering office) Building & electrical permits, final inspection. Electrical permit before work; Certificate of Final Electrical Inspection (CFEI) after — the CFEI is one of the four documents the DU requires.

§ 1Legal basis

  • RA 9136 (EPIRA, 2001) — created the ERC as the independent electricity regulator; unbundled generation / transmission / distribution.
  • RA 9513 (Renewable Energy Act, 2008), §10 — the net-metering mandate: DUs must let qualified end-users with on-grid RE systems export excess generation and receive bill credits.
  • ERC Resolution No. 09-2013 — the implementing rules: eligibility, interconnection standards (based on the Distribution Code), metering, and the credit mechanism. Amended/supplemented by the 2025–2026 issuances in §3.

§ 2Net-metering mechanics — the numbers that matter

ParameterValueEngineering / sales consequence
Residential capacity cap ≤100 kW per qualified end-user No constraint for us — a LIWANAG-class rooftop is ~2–3 kWp. (Non-residential cap lifted from 100 kW to 1 MW by DOE circular, April 2026.)
Export credit rate DU's blended generation cost — NOT the full retail rate EST Critical for the savings calculator's honesty (§1.6): a peso of self-consumed solar is worth the full retail tariff; an exported peso is worth roughly half that. Size systems for self-consumption first; never model exports at retail.
Credit carry-over Peso credits offset subsequent bills; no cash-out Frame as "lower bill," never "the utility pays you."
Metering DU replaces the meter with a bidirectional (net) meter after approval Meter swap is on the DU's clock — the one step we cannot compress; quote it as the DU's timeline, not ours.
RECs (since Oct 2025) The homeowner owns the Renewable Energy Certificates their system generates and may trade them in the Renewable Energy Market A real but small back-end line — treat as HYPOTHESIS in ops/canvas.md until a peso value at residential scale is evidenced.

§ 3The 2025–2026 reforms — why "permits handled" is now a quotable promise

The application process, historically the worst part of a residential install (months of DU back-and-forth), was overhauled in the last year. These statutory timelines are the speed-of-light constraints (§6) behind any install date we quote:

DateIssuanceWhat changed
2025-09-22 ERC advisory to all grid-connected DUs Documentary requirements standardized and cut to four items: application form · LGU-issued CFEI · amended net-metering agreement · CoC application fee.
2025-10-03 DOE Supplemental Policy End-users retain ownership of the RECs their systems generate; tradable in the Renewable Energy Market.
2026-Q1 DOE circular on processing deadlines DU must approve or reject a net-metering application within 10 working days; LGU must issue the electrical permit within 3 working days and the CFEI within 7 working days.
2026-04 DOE circular on capacity Non-residential net-metering cap raised 100 kW → 1 MW (residential stays at 100 kW).
~2026-Q3 EST Framework in progress (announced) Households not enrolling in net metering (pure self-consumption, off-grid) to need only an electrical inspection — no DU application at all.
Field-verify before quoting hard dates How smoothly the 10-day rule works varies by DU. Run one real application end-to-end with the local DU (BILECO for Biliran; Meralco if we expand) and log actual elapsed days in ops/status.md before any quote template promises a number. Until then, quote the statutory timeline with an honest stated buffer, per §6 (padding must be named).

§ 5What each rule means per package

Same regulator, three different compliance stories
LIWANAG (on-grid)ILAW (off-grid)SANDIGAN (hybrid)
Net metering Required — it is the mechanism behind the ₱-saved/month outcome. Full §4 flow applies. Not applicable — no grid export, no DU application. LGU electrical permit + inspection only (and simpler still under the ~Q3-2026 framework). Optional — net-meter the export while the battery covers backup. Full §4 flow if enrolled.
What happens in a brownout? System shuts off (anti-islanding, required by the interconnection standard). Never imply otherwise — hard stop, §1.6. Runs — it never depended on the grid. Backup circuits stay on via the battery; the grid-tied side still anti-islands.
Sales angle "Permits and net metering handled, on the DU's statutory 10-day clock." "No utility paperwork at all" — the simplest compliance story we sell. Both of the above.

§ 6Other RA 9513 mechanisms — and why they mostly don't apply to us

MechanismWhat it isRelevance
FIT (Feed-in Tariff) ERC-set guaranteed 20-year rates for utility-scale RE plants None — rooftop residential goes through net metering, not FIT.
RPS (Renewable Portfolio Standards) ERC-enforced minimum RE share DUs must source, rising yearly Indirect tailwind: DUs have a structural reason to cooperate with rooftop solar rather than obstruct it.
GEOP (Green Energy Option Program) Lets consumers with ≥100 kW demand choose an RE supplier None at residential scale; relevant only if we ever quote commercial.
REM / RECs (Renewable Energy Market) Trading venue for Renewable Energy Certificates Homeowner-owned RECs since Oct 2025 (§2) — parked as back-end HYPOTHESIS.
