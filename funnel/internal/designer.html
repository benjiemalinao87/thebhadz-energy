§ 1Reading the numbers

  • Voc / Isc — open-circuit voltage and short-circuit current: the two extremes of the IV curve. Voc sets your string sizing against inverter limits; Isc sizes fuses and connectors.
  • Vmp / Imp / Pmax — the maximum power point. Pmax = Vmp × Imp is the number on the nameplate. A real module loses 1–3% vs the sum of its cells (cell-to-module loss: ribbon resistance, mismatch) and gains some back from cell spacing and backsheet reflection.
  • Why half-cut wins. Cutting cells halves the current per cell. Resistive loss scales with I², so half current = quarter the I²R loss in ribbon — that's worth ~5–8 W on a 400 W module, plus better shade behavior because the two halves are independent parallel circuits.
  • Series × parallel. Series stacks voltage; parallel stacks current. A 108HC twin module is 54S × 2P: 54 half-cells in series per branch, two branches in parallel — same voltage as a 54-full-cell module, same current as one full cell.
  • Bypass diodes protect shaded cells from reverse-bias heating (hot spots). Each diode shorts out its column group when that group is shaded; with 3 diodes you lose ⅓ of the module to hard shade instead of all of it. Never build without them.
