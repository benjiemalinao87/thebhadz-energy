# Founder Charter — how we run this company

**Living document, owned by all founders.** The one place we write down how we work
together — culture, disagreement, decisions, hiring, growth, money, impact — so we
consult a page instead of memory. Sections are **PROPOSED (drafted 2026-07-21, ratify
or edit at a Friday meeting)** or **AGREED (date)**. Every change gets a row in the
Decision Log. The operating rules already committed to (Mission, spend gates, weekly
cadence) live in the Founder Operating System — this charter builds on it; where they
conflict, the Founder OS wins. Numbers marked ▸ are proposals to ratify, not defaults
in force.

Legal counterpart: anything that must be *enforceable* (equity, vesting, founder exit,
deadlock buyouts, IP) belongs in the Founders' Agreement — see the term sheet at
`ops/templates/founders-agreement-term-sheet.md` — not here. This charter is culture
and practice; the agreement is contract.

## 1. Mission & north star — AGREED (2026-07-11)

▸ 5 paid ₱99,500-package installs — deposits banked, not promised — by 2026-10-09.
Everything in this charter serves that number until it's met. This section exists so
the charter never grows a second north star.

## 2. Culture & values — PROPOSED

We are building a company that outlives its founders. Long-lasting means: the customer
relationship (not the sale) is the asset, the process improves every week, and the
brand is trusted in a market trained to expect scams. Five values, each with the cost
it must be allowed to charge us:

1. **Kaizen — better every install.** Every install, every quote, every ad ends with a
15-minute postmortem: one thing that goes better next time, shipped before the next
install. Root cause stated bluntly, whiteboard erased, nobody defends last week's way.
*What it costs:* time when we're busiest, and pride when the improvement contradicts a
founder's own design.
2. **The customer's bill is our scoreboard.** We measure ourselves in pesos off the
Meralco/BILECO bill, hours of light through a brownout, and referrals earned — never in
panels installed or pages shipped. Service standard: every homeowner knows their next
step (survey → design → permit → install date → energized → first lower bill) without
having to chase us, and a Messenger message gets a human reply within ▸ 4 waking hours.
*What it costs:* evenings, and saying "we were slow" out loud in the postmortem.
3. **Honest power.** We never promise what the system doesn't deliver — no brownout
implication on LIWANAG, no fabricated testimonials, no accredited-installer claims we
don't hold yet. One betrayed customer in a scam-wary province taxes every future lead.
*What it costs:* real sales, this quarter, every quarter. We pay it.
4. **Innovate below the fold.** Incumbents race specs; we innovate where the customer
actually hurts — fixed all-in price, the savings calculator, install speed, jigs and
checklists, service turnaround, honest warranty. Innovation that doesn't unblock a
buyer-experience stage is a hobby (do it on trainer-panel day).
*What it costs:* the engineer's itch to build the impressive thing instead of the
useful thing.
5. **Leave the barangay better.** Every rooftop is public proof: clean power without
genset fumes, local labor trained on our installs, batteries and e-waste disposed of
responsibly (the disposal stage is part of our offer, not the customer's problem), and
a build-log the community can read. Impact is compounding trust, not a CSR page.
*What it costs:* doing disposal and training right even when nobody would notice.

Norms already committed via the Founder OS and adopted here: bad news to the group
channel, never one-on-one; public postmortems, no defending prior work; outcomes
headline, outputs footnote; only countable commitments are evidence.

## 3. When we disagree — PROPOSED

1. **Facts are settled by tests, not seniority.** If it's checkable — a price, a claim,
a conversion guess — the cheapest 7-day real-market test or primary source decides.
2. **Domain owner decides.** The named owner (§8) hears the disagreement as 2–3 written
alternatives (no whether-or-not framing), decides against the Mission number, logs it.
3. **Disagree and commit.** Once decided, everyone executes at full effort. The
dissenter's objection is logged with a revisit-when condition — being right later is
rewarded, relitigating weekly is not.
4. **Challenge path.** A founder who thinks a decision is Mission-critical wrong writes
a one-pager; the owners meet within ▸ 48 hours; if still stuck, default to whichever
option produces a countable commitment soonest (Founder OS tie-break). Reversible +
still tied → run the ▸ ≤₱5k test; genuinely untestable → the tie-break founder named
in §8 calls it.
5. **Company-direction deadlock** (pivot, shutdown, equity, firing a founder) → the
Founders' Agreement mechanism. Invoking it is a named last resort, not a negotiating
move.
6. **How we argue:** hard on the idea in the group channel, never about the person,
nothing held for a private sidebar — no ghost grudges.

## 4. Decision making — PROPOSED

Already in force from the Founder OS: no whether-or-not decisions; reversible-in-a-week
→ Ready-Fire-Aim; irreversible → the written gates; everything logged in
`ops/status.md`.

Proposed decision tiers (₱ figures to ratify):

- **Solo (inform after):** owner's domain, spend ≤ ▸ ₱5,000, fully reversible.
- **One other founder:** spend ▸ ₱5k–₱25k, any customer-facing price/claim change, any
  new supplier commitment.
- **All founders (reserved list):** any hire or fire; single spend > ▸ ₱25k; anything
  touching the ⅓-of-cash rule; new SKU or package change; contracts > ▸ ₱50k or longer
  than 6 months; equity, debt, or founder pay; anything on the Founder OS hard-stop
  list.
- **Decision debt rule:** not written in the log within 7 days → not a decision;
  re-open it.

## 5. Hiring — PROPOSED

- **Stage One default: no specialist employees.** Contractors and per-install labor
  until the Mission is met — the first payroll hire needs the reserved list AND a
  number (installs/month) it unlocks at.
- **First critical role: licensed electrician / RME** — per-install engagement first
  (sign-off fee per install), employee only when volume sustains it. Without this role
  no deposit is legal (SC-13 §6).
- **The bar every hire clears:** a paid trial day on a real install or real funnel
  task; we hire for Kaizen fit — someone who improves the checklist beats someone who
  merely follows it; references actually called.
- **We train local.** Biliran labor first, trained on our checklists — this is the §2
  impact value operating as hiring policy, and it builds the install crew Model A will
  need.
- **Family and friends:** allowed, same bar, and the decision is made by a founder NOT
  related to them; pay and firing rules identical to anyone else.
- **Wrong hire:** acted on within ▸ 2 weeks of the postmortem naming it — slow firing
  is culture debt.

## 6. Growth strategy — PROPOSED

In force from the Founder OS: two models, one company — Model B revenue funds and gates
Model A; stage gates are numbers, never dates; one channel scaled at a time.

The NOT-doing list (each with its unlock number):

- No commercial/industrial installs until ▸ 25 residential installs are energized.
- No expansion beyond Biliran until ▸ 10 installs/month sustained 3 months (then
  adjacent municipalities by referral density, not by map ambition).
- No in-house financing/installment plans — ever on our own balance sheet; a bank/coop
  partner may be explored after ▸ 25 installs.
- No franchising talk in Stage One.
- No new SKU without its pre-registered threshold (Founder OS whole-cow rule).

**Kaizen loop on strategy itself:** after every ▸ 5 installs, re-interview the buyers
(what almost stopped you, what closed you), update `ops/positioning.md` and
`ops/canvas.md`, and re-run the §2 postmortem at company level. Long-lasting comes from
the back end: maintenance contracts, monitoring, upgrades — the install buys the
relationship; the relationship is the company.

## 7. Spending our money — PROPOSED

In force: ⅓-of-cash rule; Model A trainer cap; channel tests ≤₱12k, max 3 concurrent;
death-clock runway in `ops/status.md`.

- Spend approval follows the §4 tiers (▸ ₱5k / ₱25k).
- **Founder pay: nothing until the Mission is met.** Then an equal draw, amount set by
  reserved-list decision, revisited each quarter against the death clock.
- **Reimbursements:** receipt in the ledger within ▸ 7 days or it's a personal expense.
- **Money owner:** one named founder keeps books, ledger, death clock — and cannot
  solo-approve their own spend (the counter-signature is always someone else).
- **US↔PH money:** every peso the US-based founder puts in is documented same-week as
  either capital contribution or founder loan (decide the default with the lawyer — it
  changes equity and tax on both sides); one named founder holds in-country
  bank/GCash authority.
- Kaizen applies to cost too: after every install, one line item made cheaper or faster
  without touching the honesty/table-stakes floor.

## 9. When it's not working — PROPOSED

- **Pivot trigger:** if by the Mission date (2026-10-09) we hold fewer than ▸ 2 banked
  deposits despite ▸ ≥3 scored channel tests, the next Friday meeting is a full
  positioning review and a written compare-and-contrast of pivot options — not "one
  more month" by default.
- **Shutdown floor:** death clock at ▸ ≤2 installs of runway → all discretionary spend
  stops that day; founders decide within a week: clean wind-down or a named smaller
  shape of the company. We end honestly — customers with warranties come first in any
  wind-down.
- **Founder exit or fade:** raised at a weekly meeting, resolved within ▸ 2 weeks of
  being raised; mechanics (vesting, buyback, non-compete) come from the Founders'
  Agreement. **No ghost-founder state** — reduced involvement is renegotiated
  explicitly, never silently tolerated.

## 10. Impact — why this outlives us — PROPOSED

The impact thesis IS the business model, not a side program: affordable fixed-price
solar in brownout-hit provinces, honestly sold, locally serviced. Stage One impact
commitments (all zero-cash, all compounding):

- Train local labor on every install (§5) — skills stay in Biliran.
- Responsible battery/e-waste disposal on every job (§2, value 5).
- Build in public: the monthly founder-roof kWh/typhoon post (already mandated by the
  Founder OS) doubles as honest public education about what solar really does.
- Transparent pricing — the corridor price with its published "reason why" — as a
  market-level attack on the scam economy around solar.

Post-Mission ideas (parked until gates unlock, per Founder OS spend rules): a donated
community install per ▸ 25 paid installs (school/health center, chosen with the
barangay); scholarship or apprenticeship for a local electrician trainee. Logged here
as intent so we don't forget who we said we'd be.
