Founders' Agreement — term sheet checklist

Template only — not legal advice. This is the clause-by-clause brief to bring to a Philippine lawyer who will draft the actual Founders'/Shareholders' Agreement — the enforceable counterpart to ops/founder-charter.md. Fill the [BRACKETED] decisions before the lawyer meeting; the lawyer turns them into contract language and flags what PH law won't enforce. Where the charter is culture, this is what happens when culture isn't enough.

1. Parties & recitals

  • Founders: [NAME 1, nationality, address], [NAME 2, …], [NAME 3, …].
  • The corporation: [COMPANY NAME], Inc. (execute a short-form version pre-incorporation, then have the corporation adopt/accede after the SEC Certificate issues).
  • Foreign-equity constraint stated up front: the equity table must keep the company eligible for a PCAB regular contractor license (≥60% Filipino-owned) unless the founders explicitly decide otherwise — this binds every clause below that can move shares (vesting, buyback, transfers, dilution). A natural-born Filipino with RA 9225 reacquired citizenship counts as Filipino.

2. Equity & vesting

  • Initial split: [NAME 1: X%] / [NAME 2: Y%] / [NAME 3: Z%] — and the one-sentence rationale, written down now, while everyone still likes each other.
  • Vesting: [4] years, [1]-year cliff, monthly thereafter (the standard; PH practice implements it via share transfer/buyback mechanics rather than US-style restricted stock — let the lawyer choose the vehicle).
  • Acceleration: [full/partial] on death or permanent disability; [none] on voluntary exit; [single-trigger on sale of the company? decide].
  • Unvested shares on exit: bought back / forfeited at [par value / nominal price].

3. Roles, time & commitment

  • Full-time / part-time status of each founder and what "full-time" means given the US↔PH split (reference charter §8; the agreement enforces it, the charter operates it).
  • Officer seats per the SEC filing: President [NAME], Corporate Secretary [NAME — Filipino citizen], Treasurer [NAME — PH resident].
  • Consequence of sustained non-performance: ties into good-leaver/bad-leaver (§7), not ad-hoc punishment.

4. Capital, loans & dilution

  • Paid-in capital per founder at incorporation: [AMOUNTS] (must match the AOI Eighth article and the Treasurer's Affidavit).
  • Future money from a founder: default treatment is [capital contribution at agreed valuation / documented founder loan at [0–X]% interest] — decided per §4 reserved matters, documented same week (charter §7).
  • Pre-emptive rights: each founder may maintain their percentage in any new issuance.
  • No dividends in Stage One; distributions require unanimous consent.

5. Reserved matters (unanimous founder consent)

Mirror charter §4 and lock it contractually: new share issuance or transfer; debt above ₱[X]; any single spend above ₱[Y]; founder compensation; hiring/firing any employee; sale, merger, dissolution; amending Articles/By-laws; anything overriding a Founder OS hard stop.

6. Deadlock resolution (company-direction scale)

Escalation ladder, each step with a clock:

  1. Written one-pagers + facilitated founders' meeting within [14] days.
  2. Mediation: [30] days, mediator from [PDRCI / agreed neutral].
  3. Buy-sell ("shotgun"): either side names a per-share price; the other must buy or sell at it — with a Filipino-ownership check on the resulting cap table (§1).
  4. Arbitration: [PDRCI, Manila / alternative], governing law: Philippines, language: English. (Cross-border founders: arbitration beats courts — a PH court judgment is hard to enforce in the US and vice versa; an arbitral award travels under the New York Convention.)

7. Exit: good leaver / bad leaver

  • Good leaver (death, disability, agreed departure): keeps vested shares or sells them back at [fair value — define the formula: multiple of trailing revenue / independent valuation / book value].
  • Bad leaver (walks out pre-cliff, fraud, material breach, sustained abandonment as defined in §3): unvested forfeited; vested bought back at [par / discounted formula].
  • Company/remaining founders get first right to buy; payment terms [lump / N-month installments] so a buyback can't kill the death clock.
  • No ghost founders (charter §9): sustained non-involvement beyond [90] days without agreed leave = trigger for the §7 process.

8. Share transfer restrictions

  • Lock-in: no transfers to third parties before [the Mission is met + 12 months / DATE].
  • Right of first refusal to the company, then to founders pro-rata.
  • Tag-along (minority may join a sale) and drag-along (majority above [75]% may compel a sale) — thresholds to fit a [2–3]-founder table.
  • Any transferee must sign this agreement; spouses/heirs bound (PH conjugal property rules make this clause matter — ask the lawyer about spousal consent at signing).

9. IP assignment & confidentiality

  • All work product to date and forward — this repo, the BADJJ/Solar City brands, the designer, docs, funnel, install checklists, jigs, customer data — assigned to the corporation on incorporation; prior personal IP listed and licensed or carved out: [LIST or "none"].
  • Confidentiality survives exit by [3] years; customer list and leads are corporate assets (the Founder OS treats the lead list as an asset — the agreement makes that enforceable).

10. Non-compete / non-solicit

  • Scope proposal: no competing residential-solar install business in [Biliran + adjacent provinces] for [2] years post-exit; no soliciting employees, contractors, or customers for [2] years.
  • PH reality check for the lawyer: post-employment/post-exit restraints are enforceable only if reasonable in time, territory, and trade — get the narrowest version that actually protects the company, not a broad one a court voids entirely.

11. Boilerplate the lawyer completes

Insurance on founders [key-person? decide]; notices (email + Viber/WhatsApp valid); amendment only in writing, unanimous; severability; entire agreement; relationship to By-laws (this agreement prevails between the founders to the extent law allows — by-laws stay the public/legal minimum); counterparts + electronic signature; apostille plan for any founder signing in the US.
