2026-07-13 · Meeting #3

Capital structure & governance

Benjie Malinao · Ace Denver Corpin
Jethro Abatias

Follow-up meeting focused on formalizing the share structure, ownership split, and corporate governance model. Ace (as treasurer-designate) walked through the accounting mechanics of authorized vs. issued vs. unissued shares; the group also revisited the business name question and discussed how customer support should be staffed in the early stage.

Discussion

Business name. Revisited naming direction — leaning toward a name that reads as a long-term electrical / energy systems and engineering company rather than something narrowly "solar," consistent with the kickoff meeting's direction. Agreed Benjie will bring 5 candidate names to the next meeting, each with a one-to-two-minute rationale, and the group will cross off down to one final choice.

Capital stock & shares. Ace proposed an authorized capital stock of 100,000 shares (par value one peso per share, so 100,000 shares = ₱100,000 total authorized capital) — a structure he noted is typical for tech/energy startups. Discussed splitting the initial issuance across the founders (original proposal ~40,000 shares issued, 10,000 each across 4 founders). Discussion moved the issued amount to 60,000 shares issued (₱60,000), leaving 40,000 shares unissued as a reserve for future investors/equity funding. Ownership percentage is calculated against total issued shares, not the full 100,000 authorized — e.g. 60,000 shares from a founder's contribution nets out to roughly 1.6% per ₱1,000 put in at this stage, which compounds significantly as the company grows.

Ownership split. Went back and forth on ratios (60/40, 50/50, 80/20 scenarios) for how issued shares divide among founders/investors. Benjie's framing: because the company is private (not publicly traded), the exact founder split matters less than everyone agreeing and moving forward — a small % now can become a large absolute value later as the "pie" grows. No final ratio locked in; flagged as something to close out next meeting.

Accounting treatment. The ₱60,000 issued capital becomes the corporation's initial working capital once the corporate bank account is opened. Any founder contribution beyond a person's subscribed share amount is booked as a director's/shareholder loan (a liability payable to that founder), not additional equity — keeps capital contributions and loans cleanly separated on the books.

Governance structure. Walked through the standard PH corporate hierarchy: Shareholders → Board of Directors → Corporate Officers (President, Corporate Secretary, Treasurer) → executive departments. Shareholders must hold an annual meeting at minimum and vote on things like amending the articles of incorporation or increasing authorized capital. The board formally approves annual budget, corporate loans, major capital expenditure, major commercial contracts, and C-level hiring — but Benjie flagged that in practice, at this size, the group will keep deciding by full-founder roundtable/consensus rather than leaning on formal protocol, to stay fast. Tentatively floated Jundhel (Junel) for President given his availability to lead the first registration filing; Corporate Secretary and Treasurer roles still to be confirmed.

Customer support philosophy. Benjie's position: in the early stage, all founders handle customer support directly rather than delegating it — staying closest to the customer is how the team feels real pain points and improves faster. Framed the broader competitive category as "home improvement" (roofing, solar installation, windows, bathroom remodeling), where being personally responsive on the first handful of clients builds the trust and expertise the team will later formalize into a training/certification playbook for hires.

Process note. Confirmed the meeting recording + AI-generated summary/minutes workflow (this log) as the standing process going forward.

✓ Decisions
  • Authorized capital stock: 100,000 shares (₱1 par value/share).
  • Move to 60,000 shares issued (₱60,000 initial capital), 40,000 shares held unissued as an investor/equity reserve.
  • Ownership % is calculated against issued shares, not the full authorized pool.
  • Contributions beyond a founder's subscribed shares are booked as a director's loan (liability), not equity.
  • Governance follows standard shareholders → board → officers structure on paper, but day-to-day decisions stay full-founder consensus while the team is this small.
  • Meeting recording + AI-generated minutes confirmed as the standing process.

Brand name. Debated Power Tech, Power City, and Solar City. Consensus: the vision is bigger than solar — energy storage, EV charging, gas/charging stations — so the name shouldn't box us into "solar" only. Resolved after meeting → BADJJ Engineering & Energy Systems Inc.

Culture. Full alignment, honesty, no talking behind each other's backs. Everyone wears multiple hats early; Benjie leaning into sales & marketing (location, market growth).
