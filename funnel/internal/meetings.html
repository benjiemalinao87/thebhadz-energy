2026-07-06 · Meeting #1

Founders' kickoff

Benjie Malinao · Ace Denver Corpin
Jundhel Cabradilla · Jethro Abatias

First alignment meeting for the company. Covered how we legally structure the business, how equity and funding work, the initial capital to get moving, and the brand direction. Tone was long-term and committed — "we're building a billion-dollar company, so we have to be aligned 100%, honest, no gossip."

Discussion

Legal structure. Ace made the case for a corporation / incorporation over a sole proprietorship (which legally auto-dissolves) or a simple partnership. The power sector is a billion-dollar industry; a corporation is a proper legal entity that can scale, hire employees, and issue shares. A PH corporation traditionally needs at least 5 founders filling officer roles — President (board member) Secretary Treasurer — plus a corporate bank account.

Equity & ownership. Equal ownership among founders at the start, proportional to capital contributed. Worked through examples (e.g. ₱100k capital → ₱50k/₱50k = 50/50, or split across 4). Agreed to set aside a dedicated company development fund — a "slice of the pie" — so the company itself can fund salespeople and development, which is how it grows.

Brand name. Debated Power Tech, Power City, and Solar City. Consensus: the vision is bigger than solar — energy storage, EV charging, gas/charging stations — so the name shouldn't box us into "solar" only. Resolved after meeting → BADJJ Engineering & Energy Systems Inc.

Culture. Full alignment, honesty, no talking behind each other's backs. Everyone wears multiple hats early; Benjie leaning into sales & marketing (location, market growth).

✓ Decisions
  • Form a corporation (not sole prop / plain partnership).
  • Equal ownership, proportional to capital in; create a company development fund.
  • Initial funding: ₱30,000 to start requirements (permit, registration).
  • Name direction not limited to "solar" → became BADJJ Energy Systems.
