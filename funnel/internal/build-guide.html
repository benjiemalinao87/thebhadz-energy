SC-02 · BUILD GUIDE · REV A · 2026-07-06

Hand-building a module

The full bench process: from loose cells to a framed, tested module. Written for the 108-half-cell 400 W target from SC-01, with notes where the 36-cell trainer differs. Read the whole document once before touching a cell.

Build the trainer first Your first module should be the 100 W 36-cell trainer (SC-01 preset). Same process, same failure modes, one-tenth the cost per mistake. Broken cells and bad solder joints are tuition — pay it on ₱3,000 of cells, not ₱15,000 of TOPCon.
Safety — read before step 1 A 108HC module in sunlight produces ~38 V at up to 14 A — it cannot be "switched off." Cover the module with opaque sheet whenever leads are exposed. Soldering: fume extraction or strong cross-draft; flux fumes are respiratory irritants. Lamination: 150 °C surfaces and hot EVA; heat gloves, no loose sleeves. Hi-pot testing (step 12) applies up to 3 kV DC — one person on the test, hands off the module, test area barricaded.

§ 0Workspace and tooling

Minimum bench for prototypes: a flat, clean, dry room you can keep dust-low (cells and EVA hate dust and moisture); a 2 × 1.5 m work table with soft ESD-safe covering; nitrile gloves — skin oils degrade cell surfaces and EVA adhesion.

01

Receive and grade cells

~2 h / batch

Cells arrive in vacuum-sealed bricks of 100–200. They are 130–170 µm thick — they flex once, then crack. Handle by edges, gloves on, one at a time.

  • Inspect every cell on a light table or against a bright window: chips, micro-cracks (fine dark lines), finger interruptions.
  • Sort by color and by binned current class if the supplier mixed bins — mismatched cells drag the whole string to the worst cell's current.
  • Store rejects: chipped cells are fine for practice soldering.
QC gate Reject rate over 3% on arrival → photograph and claim against the supplier immediately. Log every cell batch: supplier, bin, date, reject count.
02

Cut cells (half-cut builds only)

~3 h / 60 cells

Best option: buy cells already half-cut — most distributors sell them, and factory laser scribing beats anything you can do by hand. If you must cut:

  • Score with a fiber laser (20–30 W, single pass along the designated scribe lane on the rear) to ~⅓ depth, then cleave over a straight edge. This is "scribe and break."
  • Do not use a hand diamond scribe on modern PERC/TOPCon cells except for practice — edge micro-cracks will show up as dark regions in EL later.
CUT LANE: across busbars, exactly between the two half-cell busbar patterns — M10 cells are printed for halving; the lane is visible.
EDGE QC: cleaved edge must be straight and shiny; jagged = reject for module use.
03

Tab the cells

~10 min/cell hand · the skill step

Tabbing = soldering ribbon along each busbar on the cell front, with a tail hanging off to reach the back of the next cell. This is 80% of hand-build quality. Practice on rejects until a ribbon peels glass-side silicon rather than lifting off (peel strength > 1 N/mm means the joint is stronger than the print).

  1. Cut ribbon lengths = 2 × cell height + 5 mm (covers this cell's front + next cell's back).
  2. Run the flux pen along each busbar. Thin, even, no pooling.
  3. Lay ribbon on the busbar, hold with a weighted guide, draw the iron once from center outward each way at a steady ~10 mm/s. The ribbon's solder coating does the joining — no added solder.
  4. No cold joints (dull, blobby) and no scorched cells (browned print).
IRON: 340–360 °C chisel tip · RIBBON: SnPb-coated (Sn60Pb40 coat, melts ~183 °C) — start with leaded coating; lead-free (SnAgCu, ~220 °C) is harder to hand-solder · FLUX: no-clean rosin, Kester 951-class · PASSES: one. Re-working a joint twice = scrap the cell.
QC gate Random 1-in-10 peel test on tabbed rejects. Visual: ribbon centered ±0.5 mm, no solder balls, no crack sounds during handling (yes, you can hear them).
04

String the cells

~1 h / string of 18

Connect tabbed cells front-to-back in series: this cell's front ribbons solder to the next cell's rear pads. Build on a stringing jig — a board with stops that set the 2 mm cell gap repeatably.

  • Polarity discipline: front = negative, rear = positive on most mono cells. Every string flows the same way; label string ends with tape flags (+/−).
  • After each string: multimeter Voc in daylight ≈ N × 0.6–0.72 V. An 18-half-cell string should read ~12–13 V in good sun.
  • Lift finished strings with a thin board underneath, never by the end cells.
TARGET (108HC): 6 strings × 18 half-cells · GAP: 2.0 ± 0.5 mm · TRAINER: 4 strings × 9 full cells.
05

Prepare glass and cut sheets

~1 h
  • Glass: 3.2 mm low-iron tempered, cut-to-size with edges seamed (they arrive that way — you cannot cut tempered glass). Wash with IPA/water 50:50, lint-free wipes, dry fully. The tin side (test with UV lamp) faces out; AR coating faces the sun.
  • Cut two EVA sheets and one backsheet 10–20 mm oversize on all edges. Blades, not scissors — clean edges.
  • Keep EVA sealed until use; it absorbs moisture and has a shelf life (~6 months at <30 °C). Note the lot number in the build log.
06

Layup

~2 h

Assemble the sandwich face-down (sun side down): glass → EVA → cell matrix (face down) → EVA → backsheet. This is the exploded diagram on SC-00 made real.

  1. Glass on padded table, washed side up (inner surface).
  2. First EVA sheet, embossed side per manufacturer's spec.
  3. Place strings face-down using a placement template. String spacing 2 mm; laminate edge margin ≥ 15 mm on every side (we use 20/25 — see SC-01 dimensions).
  4. Solder bus ribbons across string ends to wire the series/parallel topology and the diode taps from your SC-01 map. Insulate any bus crossing a cell edge with a patch of backsheet or PET tape.
  5. For half-cut twin: center bus taps exit through slits cut in the rear EVA + backsheet at the module centerline; for the trainer: two end taps exit at the top edge.
  6. Second EVA, then backsheet. Final visual sweep for debris — a hair laminated into the stack is a permanent void.
QC gate Pre-lamination EL or at minimum a dark-room forward-bias IR check (power the layup at low current, look for cold cells with an IR camera). This is the last cheap moment to fix anything.
07

Pre-lamination electrical check

15 min
Voc CHECK: full layup in daylight should read the SC-01 predicted Voc ±5% (~38 V for 108HC). POLARITY: confirm + and − leads against the diode orientation plan. A reversed diode survives lamination and then shorts your module forever.
08

Laminate

~25 min cycle + cooldown

Lamination melts and cross-links the EVA into a permanent, optically clear, waterproof bond. The three paths, in order of preference:

  • Benchtop/workshop laminator — vacuum membrane press with heated platen. The correct tool; small used/new units exist sized for one module (see SC-03/SC-04).
  • DIY vacuum-bag rig — silicone membrane or nylon bag + venturi/rotary vane pump + a flat heated platen (or insulated box with the layup on an aluminum plate over heating elements). Works for trainer-size; hard to keep ±5 °C uniformity at full 1.7 m module size.
  • No lamination (trainer only, temporary) — frame + silicone edge seal without encapsulant is a science-fair panel: it will fail in months outdoors. Acceptable only for indoor electrical learning.
FAST-CURE EVA RECIPE (typical — follow your EVA datasheet over this):
1. PUMP-DOWN: chamber to <1 mbar, platen 145–150 °C, 4–6 min vacuum, membrane up (no press).
2. PRESS: membrane down at ~50–80 kPa, hold 8–12 min at 145–155 °C.
3. VENT + COOL: release, move to cooling rack, flat, no handling until <50 °C.
CURE QC: gel content >75% (lab test) — bench proxy: a sacrificial EVA coupon from the same run should not dissolve/swell apart in toluene overnight.
Failure modes Bubbles = pump-down too short or moist EVA. Cell cracks = pressed too early/too hard. Milky EVA = under-cured. Yellowing later = over-cured or bad EVA. Log every cycle: temps, times, vacuum level, EVA lot.
09

Trim and frame

~1 h
  • Trim overhanging EVA/backsheet flush with a blade.
  • Fill the frame channel with neutral-cure silicone (or fit butyl tape), press frame profiles on, join corners with corner keys. Clamp square; check diagonals equal within 2 mm.
  • Neutral-cure only — acetoxy silicone (vinegar smell) corrodes cell metallization and the frame.
10

Junction box and leads

~45 min + cure
  • Solder the exit taps to the J-box terminals — diode orientation per the SC-01 map (cathode band toward positive). For half-cut: three split boxes along the centerline. Trainer: one box, top rear.
  • Seat the box on silicone, then pot the cavity with the two-part potting silicone supplied for the box. Potting is the module's main moisture seal around live parts — no voids.
  • Dress MC4 pigtails; genuine-brand or first-tier connectors only. Counterfeit MC4s are a fire origin, not a savings.
11

Test: performance

~30 min
  • Outdoor IV curve: clear noon sun, module at normal incidence, log irradiance (a calibrated reference cell or even a known-good commercial module beside it) and cell temperature. Translate to STC and compare against SC-01: Pmax within −8%/+3% of prediction is a good hand build.
  • EL image: forward-bias the module at ~Isc in a dark room; a modified (IR-filter-removed) mirrorless camera sees cell electroluminescence at ~1150 nm. Dark cells, lightning-bolt cracks, dead fingers — all visible. This is the single most informative image of your build quality.
12

Test: safety, then log and label

~30 min
  • Insulation (hi-pot): leads shorted together vs frame, 500 V DC insulation test first (>40 MΩ·m² per IEC 61215 scaling), then if equipped, 3 kV DC dielectric withstand — 1 min, no breakdown. Wet leakage (module in a shallow conductive water bath, frame vs leads) is the certification-grade version.
  • Ground continuity: frame-to-frame-bolt path < 0.1 Ω.
  • Serialize the module (engraved tag or laminated label): SN, date, cell lot, EVA lot, lamination cycle ID, measured Pmax/Voc/Isc/Vmp/Imp. This log is the seed of your factory MES — start the habit at module #1.
Exit criteria — stage 1 complete A module that: passes EL with no new cracks vs pre-lam, holds hi-pot, measures within −8% of SC-01 prediction, and survives 4 weeks outdoors with <1% power change re-measured. Build 3–5 to this standard, then open SC-04.
